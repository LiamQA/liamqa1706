Lingston Celebrates National Unity Day

In a grand ceremony, citizens across Lingston came together to celebrate National Unity Day. The festivities included cultural performances, community events, and a speech by HSC. Quentin Aguda, emphasizing the importance of unity in nation-building.

Parliment Reviews the EDA Act of 2020

Eighteen nations have now joined the Economic and Diplomacy Alliance Act of 2020 following its highly successful passage. and obtained all of the advantages specified by the Act. Nonetheless, as a result of the Act's Visa-free advantages, Lingston has obtained almost 600,000 newcomers Since the Act's passage, the number of migrants has expanded dramatically, driving up rent prices and driving up demand for housing and employment. Prime Minister Ballestors has called on parliament to reform the EDA Act in order to address this problem. He has also mandated that the Ministry of the Interior select visa applicants more carefully, with a yearly maximum of 3,000.

The National Justice Tribunal warrants the arrest of 4 Parliment members

Five days following the repeal of the Civil Order statute, the National Justice Tribunal issued a warrant for the arrest of four members of parliament on charges of treason against the country and disruption of civil order. The Tribunal chose to keep their identities confidential for the time being and did not reveal who they were.

The NSC (National Supreme Council) votes to evokes Article 8, The Civil Order law

Because of recent violent riots and public unrest, the NSC resolved to activate the public Order statute and impose a countrywide state of emergency. Following the passage of the bill, the Old Guard and the National Defence Force were dispatched to the capital city and evacuated the Aguda family, as well as members of the Parliament and the Supreme Council. Riots erupt as Bill 12-23 passes the voting procedure in Parliament and awaits approval from the Supreme Council. Approving the Bill will grant the extension of certain Parliment members terms to 4 more years. This Bill has sparked outrage from the general public including a condemnation from the NSC in an X post, which HSC. Quentin states in the following, "The Supreme Council of Lingston vehmently condemns Parliment's recent decision to pass a bill granting select members a 4-year term extension. This action flagrantly goes against the principles of our constitution, undermining the democratic process."
